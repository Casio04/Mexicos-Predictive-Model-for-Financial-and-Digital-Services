Machine Learning Model

Description

Having in mind the information and patterns we saw in the previous dashboards, the next step was training a model with all the information available and considering the relationships we identified among every gathered variable in the digital and financial environment up to 2020.
The fundamental question that we answer with a machine learning model is:
Which is the predicted effect of financial and digital variables based on changes in other variables?
For instance, which is the effect of an increase in computer consumption in a municipality over streaming subscriptions?

Model Results

We tested our model for the 5 variables presented, obtaining the following results:

* The Mean Square Error (MSE) is the average squared difference between the estimated values and the actual value. This is a measure of the quality of our estimations. It means that values closer to zero are better.
** The coefficient of detemination (R2) tells how good is the model to explain the changes in the variable tested. The strength of the relationship between your model and the dependent variable goes from 0 to 1, where 1 is a perfect fit. In marketing research, R2 values of 0.75, 0.50, 0.25 are considered as substantial, moderate or weak. Source

Heat Map

Heatmap shows us the direct or indirect relationship of each variable with every other from the data. In this case, the yellow square means a perfect 1 to 1 relationship, meaning that when X variable (e.g. PC) increases in 1% from its previous value, the other related variable (e.g. Internet) increases in the same proportion. The machile learning model uses variables from the upper-right quadrant, which are variables from financial services compared to variables of digital services.
