MACHINE LEARNING MODEL

What is Machine Learning?

Having in mind the information and patterns we saw in the previous dashboards, the next step was to train a model with all the information available and considering the relationships we identified among every digital and/or financial service
The fundamental question that we answer with this specific machine learning model is:
Which is the predicted effect of financial and digital variables based on changes in other variables?
For instance, which is the effect of an increase in computer consumption in a municipality over streaming subscriptions?

Model Efficiency

We tested our model for the 5 variables presented, obtaining the following results:

* The Mean Square Error (MSE) is the average squared difference between the estimated values and the actual value. This is a measure of the quality of our estimations. It means that values closer to zero are better.
** The coefficient of detemination (R2) tells how good is the model to explain the changes in the variable tested. The strength of the relationship between your model and the dependent variable goes from 0 to 1, where 1 is a perfect fit. In marketing research, R2 values of 0.75, 0.50, 0.25 are considered as substantial, moderate or weak. Source

Heat Map - Digital vs Financial Services in Mexico 2020

Heatmap shows us the direct or indirect relationship of each variable with every other from the data. In this case, the yellow square means a perfect 1 to 1 relationship, meaning that when X variable (e.g. PC) increases in 1% from its previous value, the other related variable (e.g. Internet) increases in the same proportion. This can quickly tell us how related (or not) are two variables between them.

What is (DEM)? DEM stands for demographic variable, meaning that it is a calculation of density from that specific service. All the DEM variables are calculated on a ratio 1:1, meaning that for each adult person, there is X units of that specific service (i.e. A value of 0.5 in Credit Card would mean that, on such municipality, for every 2 people there is, on average, 1 issued credit card)
